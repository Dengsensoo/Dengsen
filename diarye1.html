This diary begins with the journey from Korea to Malaysia on September 20th.
My diary of being discharged from the military in March and living in Europe until August is saved on the ‘Densensoo’ Naver blog.
I returned to Korea from France in early August, spent about a month and a half reorganizing, and then prepared for a trip to Southeast Asia. The first destination was decided to be Malaysia.
The reason is that there are many opportunities to use English, and prices are expensive compared to other Southeast Asian countries.
I decided this was a good place to start because it was as safe as it was expensive.



September 20
After arriving at Incheon Airport on an airport bus that I don't know why it costs 17,000 won, I was told that in-flight meals on the flight to Malaysia are paid, so I ate kimchi stew at the airport restaurant, which I might miss later.
So I took an evening flight and arrived in Malaysia around midnight.


September 21
I stayed at a pre-booked accommodation near the Malaysian airport for 3 days and 2 nights, but there was a problem.
Since the airport is large, the hotel is located far away, so even if you take a taxi, it will be quite far.
Since it was a hotel next to the airport building, there wasn't much to see.
I could talk to people, eat, and enjoy the natural scenery.
One day, I toured the airport and it felt like I was all over the world, so I feel good.
But what I'm looking forward to is the moment I check in to the accommodation I booked around KL Sentral in the city and wander around the city.


September 22
I’m excited to take the KLIA Express (Airport Railroad) to Kuala Lumpur.
The hotel check-in time was 3pm, so I left my luggage at KL Sentral until then
and took a walk around the area, which turned out to be Little India.
After riding the monorail, I got off at Hang Tuah Station and walked around, and there was street food for sale.
However, when I tried to withdraw cash in 100 ringgit increments, it didn't seem to work,
so I just watched with my eyes. I almost swore. So, I went back to KL Sentral
and exchanged most of the 100 ringgit notes for 50 ringgit at the currency exchange booth.

Anyway, now that I check in to the hotel, I'm starting my trip to Kuala Lumpur in earnest.
I went out and went around and ate an Indian food called Cheese Naan at a restaurant, and it was so delicious.
I walked straight to Masjid Jamek via Pasar Seni. It feels amazing and fascinated.
It's safer than I thought. Is it because it is a city where various cultures coexist,
along with the feeling that there is everything from a traveler's point of view compared to Europe?


September 23
I had breakfast for hotel breakfast, but it's cheap because the normal meal is 10RM.
I went to Chinatown and it was in the form of a marketplace and there are many things to buy. Bags, clothes, cases, etc...
I ate dessert and iced white coffee was delicious at a special cafe,
so I thought it was a waste of money I spent in Europe.


September 24
I've also been to Bukit Bintang, and I could see many young people hanging out.
There were people singing, there were kebab restaurants, and many foreign restaurants.
And there were many shopping malls, so I walk with air conditioning and go to the bathroom.
In the evening, I had a meal eating something like Chapati and sauces on a banana leaf at an Indian restaurant.


September 25
I went to Petronas Twin Towers where were promoted by media. it's a shopping mall feels like Lotte Tower.
It's a place people who want to know Malaysia don't have to go.
So I took the subway toward the city and I got off in the middle. While I was walking, I saw many houses with murals.
I went to the airport to meet my mom at night. I went to there by airport bus,
It was definitely much cheaper and more cost-effective than the airport railway.


September 26
엄마를 데리고 KLCC 쇼핑몰로 들어가서 명품들을 보게 했는데 엄마는 명품에 홀리지 않았다.
사진을 많이 안 찍어놔서 어디를 갔는지 기억이 안 나는데 어쨌든 이 날은 많이 다녔다.


September 27
조금 시골쪽으로 가서 걸어봤다. 가다가 식당을 들어가서 시원한 과일주스에 얼음을 탄 비닐봉투에
빨대를 꽂아주고 그 밑에 바구니를 놔서 주니까 더 시원하게 느껴졌다.
이 날은 다음날 숙소를 미리 예약을 안해놔서 주구장창 호텔을 알아보러 다녔다.

부킷 빈탕 주변에 분위기 재밌는 중국인 거리에 있는 숙소도 알아봤지만 창문이 없었고
그렇게 다니다가 쿠알라 룸푸르를 벗어나 프탈링자야라는 서쪽의 도시에서 숙소를 잡아봤다.
숙소를 잡을 때 그 장소를 직접 가보고 숙소 사정도 함께 보는 습관이 이 때 생겼다.


September 28
여러모로 조건이 괜찮은 호텔이였고 시내까지 그랩택시를 타서 가야한다는 점만 단점이였다.
이 Centrestage Petaling Jaya라는 이름의 숙소 주변환경은 쇼핑센터와 민가가 있었다.
좀 더 벗어나면 전철역도 있는데, 가는 길에는 집같은 집. 단독주택이 많이 보였다.
전철타고 3정거장 간격으로 내렸다 걷다 다시 타보고 그랬는데 나쁘지 않은 재미였다.
자야 쇼핑센터 4층에 가서 엄마를 만났고 영화관 구경, 같이 디저트와 초코쉐이크도 마셨다.

저녁에는 시내로 가서 Central Market도 갔고 그곳은 영화적인 노래와 함께 신기한걸 많이 팔았다.
옷, 비누, 그림, 카페트 등등...내가 이 도시에 집이 있다면 사고싶은 물건들이였다.


September 29
아침에 산책을 하는데 고양이 밥 먹길래 구경하게 됐다. 이건 솔직히 영상으로 설명하는게 제격
그동안 매일 연속적으로 밖을 다녔어서 그런지 몸이 피곤해서 낮까지 호텔에서 쉬었다.
저녁에 리틀인디아를 엄마랑 같이 갔는데 노란 꽃들이 매달린 거리를 걸었고, 꽃을 하나 샀다
그러다가 식당에서 밥을 먹다가 옆에 앉아있는 몸 좋은 여자한테 자꾸 눈길이 갔는데

그건 그렇고 중요한건 리틀인디아는 화려한 옷들과 옷감을 많이 팔고 있었는데
여기서 돈을 제법 썼던 것 같다. 정말 평생 못 입어볼 뻔한 무늬의 옷들이 많았다.
'Sari'는 옷감 그대로 입는 옷인거 같은데 엄마가 입어보고 나는 그걸 찍어서 보내드렸다.
돌아가기 전에는 Mix Fruit Lassi랑 망고라씨 Mango Lassi를 마셨던 것 같은데
다른 일반적인 식당에서 마셨던 것보다 맛있어서 질이 높은 식당에서 마셔야겠다.


September 30
전철타고 외곽지역을 갔는데 한국으로 치면 경기도를 갔다고 할 수 있는데
그 곳에서는 시내에선 잘 안보이던 단독주택이 많이 보인다. 더 멀리가면 외지도 보이겠지?
어쨌든 이곳도 살만한 동네인 것 같다. 시내쪽으로 다시 돌아가서 갈색 반팔티 1만원짜리도 샀다.

저녁에는 호텔에서 나는 엄마한테 말레이시아에서 주로 뭐 해야 하는지에 대한 얘기를 했는데
그 때 엄마가 좋은 얘기를 해줘서 나는 코딩실력을 쌓는 첫 관문으로 내 사이트를 만들기로 결심했다.
그 동안 기록은 하면 좋고 안하면 아쉬운 정도로 알고 있었지만
여러 나라를 다닐 때 기록이 없다는 것은 있을 수 없는 일로 여기게 된다.
